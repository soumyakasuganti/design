Challenge


Bookings in the existing system were hard to access. Furthermore, the system provided limited visibility of status and little or no freedom to manage bookings. Consequently, most requests / issues were handled by the customer service team, causing heavy loads and long wait times.


Task


Designing a comprehensive booking management system, planned in phases, giving the user complete visibility and control over their bookings and refunds.


My Role


Design lead

Outlining the post booking experience

How I Contributed


I designed the post booking experience for the flights product, starting with a flowchart to identify task flows and back-end processes. I juxtaposed these with low fidelity wireframes to facilitate a strategy workshop. The outcome was a shared vision for the project, a roadmap, and an understanding of potential issues. With the roadmap in place, I designed and tested prototypes for the first two phases. These became a part of the design system and have subsequently been used across multiple products.

Low fidelity wireframes

Design Decisions


I proposed that we have separate booking confirmation and manage booking experiences based on user goals at these stages. The confirmation page would provide a brief summary of the booking, and focus on cross-selling our other offerings. The manage booking page on the other hand would focus on booking details and the options available to the user.

I provided visibility for failed bookings, and added a tracker allowing users to stay updated on their requests and refunds.

I also proposed that we display the major modification options earlier in the user flow, allowing users to differentiate between a partial and complete cancellation, a refund for a cancelled flight, etc.

Learnings and Opportunities


Visualizing the end-to-end flow and getting stakeholders involved early helped set expectations and identify potential issues upfront.

COVID 19 had a significant impact on the travel industry, leading to some modifications to the roadmap. This has allowed additional time for heuristic evaluation and usability testing.
