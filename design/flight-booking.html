  • — Redesigning the booking Experience

Context

JetPrivilege, previously Jet Airways' loyalty program, became an independent entity. Members could now earn and redeem miles on any airline.


Task

Redesigning the booking experience to reflect the new value proposition and brand identity, benchmarking against common OTA and airline user experiences.


My Role

Designer (pair design), design lead.


Overview of process and participants

Task flow, split into phases

Strategy


The business was undergoing a major shift, and the product needed to go to market fast. The decision was made to release an MVP and subsequently carry out research and testing. There was still a need to meet customer expectations, so we analyzed a selected list of booking platforms, noting common practices and possible areas of improvement.


Discovery and Ideation


Equipped with insights from the analysis, we mapped the user flow, outlined alternatives, and fleshed them out as wireframes. We then conducted a workshop, soliciting feedback from the product and leadership teams. The feasibility of the proposed solution(s) was also checked at this point.

Phase 1 — Search Widget


In addition to the expected widget behaviour, we worked on


  • Communicating the value proposition upfront
  • Providing a clear choice between earning and redeeming intermiles
  • Remembering recent searches
  • Making the one way / round trip choice part of date selection

Phase 2 — Search Results


The search results proved to be challenging to do well within the 3 week time frame. We decided to focus on


  • Highlighting the miles earning for each flight
  • Enabling selection of onward and return flights simultaneously
  • Displaying filters prominently
  • Showing fares for dates surrounding the chosen date(s) on desktop

Phase 3 — Booking Review


During this phase I took over as design lead and worked on the project independently. I focused on simplifying the journey as much as possible


  • Making flight information easy to scan
  • Highlighting halt / layover information
  • Minimizing the traveller information collected
  • Collapsing infrequently used sections, such as frequent flier and GST
  • Simplifying title input on mobile using chips
  • Providing greater control in the add-ons sections using tabs
  • Enabling the skip option for add-ons

The company rebranded at this time, leading to added complexity during prototyping. I sought additional feedback from the design team to ensure consistency was maintained.

Learnings


Usability testing earlier in the process would have helped significantly. Though we did some testing internally, guerilla research methods might have helped achieve a better outcome at the MVP stage.

The number of stakeholders involved in this project made decision making somewhat challenging at times. I used the experience to modify my process in subsequent projects, making sure that user experience decisions and feasibility checks were handled and documented early on in the process.

Given the changes to the team and the business during the course of the project, I had to handle a certain level of ambiguity, deal with changes to the scope of work, and manage my time with greater skill.
